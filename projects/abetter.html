Product Ranking Prediction & Comparative Advantage Analysis

ABetter × Coupang | Jul 2025 – Aug 2025

Data Analysis Time Series Machine Learning Deep Learning

Project Overview

This project aims to analyze comparative advantages of e-commerce products and to build ranking prediction models for search result exposure on Coupang. The work combines econometric analysis, time-series modeling, and learning-to-rank models.

1. Comparative Advantage Analysis

1-1. Price Change vs Order Volume
  • Panel OLS with Fixed / Random Effects
  • Target: 7-day rolling order volume

Key Findings

  • Recent daily / 7-day price changes → Not significant
  • Previous day’s price level → Statistically significant but minimal effect
1-2. Negative Review Ratio vs CVR

Applied a Single Turning Point Regression to detect CVR decline thresholds.

y = a + b₁x + b₂ · max(0, x − τ)
  • Analyzed products with ≥100 reviews & ≥100 impressions
  • 7 out of 37 products showed a clear CVR drop after a threshold
  • No universal threshold → product-specific risk levels
1-3. Effectiveness of Review Actions
  • Separated advertising reviews from organic reviews
  • Defined Ad Review Ratio threshold
  • Measured pure sales excluding ad-induced effects

Result: Short-term sales uplift observed, but effects do not persist long-term.

2. Search Volume & Sales Dynamics

  • Time-series decomposition (Trend / Seasonality / Residual)
  • Automatic tagging of demand drivers
  • ADF Test, Residual ACF, CCF, Granger, ARDL

Products were classified into A–F groups based on short-term predictability and long-term elasticity between search volume and sales.

This framework enables distinguishing between base-demand changes and temporary traffic spikes.

3. Product Ranking Modeling

3-1. ML Baseline (LightGBM)
  • Multi-class classification (Top 10 / 11–36 / 37+)
  • Keyword-specific feature selection (Backward Elimination)
  • Optuna-based hyperparameter tuning

Macro F1-score: 0.69
Key drivers: past performance, relative keyword positioning

3-2. DL Model (Position-Aware ListNet)
  • Learning-to-Rank (Listwise)
  • Category & temporal embeddings
  • Position-aware ListMLE loss

Macro F1-score: 0.86
Significant improvement over ML baseline despite limited data.

Conclusion & Impact

  • Established a systematic framework for evaluating action effectiveness
  • Enabled pre-estimation of ranking uplift potential
  • Provided decision-support signals for advertising & pricing strategies

Keywords: Learning to Rank, Time Series Analysis, Causal Inference, E-commerce Analytics
