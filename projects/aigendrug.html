Aigendrug · ADME-based Drug-likeness Prediction

Deep Learning Approach for Compound Classification & Property Prediction

PyTorch RDKit PyG (Graph) DeepChem Bioinformatics

Project Overview

The goal of Aigendrug is to develop an AI algorithm that predicts ADME (Absorption, Distribution, Metabolism, Excretion) properties of novel compounds without direct experimentation, subsequently evaluating their potential drug-likeness.

1. Approach & Architecture

1-1. Molecular Representation
  • Input: Conversion of chemical compounds into Canonical SMILES.
  • Preprocessing: Transformation into Graph structures and Fingerprint representations.
1-2. Multi-Task Learning Pipeline

We employed a two-stage learning process to maximize feature utility:

  1. Encoder Training: Learn ADME-aware embeddings by training on specific property tasks.
  2. Drug Compound Classification (DCC): Leverage the learned embeddings to classify overall drug-likeness.

Key Findings

  • Multi-task learning improves performance across all DCC tasks.

3. Validation & Demo Cases

PCA-based visualization was used to verify the relative positioning of new compounds within the learned drug-like embedding space.

Case Studies
  • Acetaminophen: Correctly classified as a drug with high probability.
  • Tetrodotoxin: Correctly identified as a non-drug (toxin) compound.

Conclusion

  • Successfully established an end-to-end pipeline for molecular property prediction.
  • Validated that transferring ADME-specific knowledge enhances general drug-likeness classification.
