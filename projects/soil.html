S-OIL · Fuel Demand Forecasting & Network Strategy

Data-Driven Demand Forecasting & Gas Station Network Optimization

Time Series Analysis Econometrics Compositional Data Analysis Spatial Optimization

Project Overview

This project aimed to strengthen S-OIL’s retail business competitiveness by forecasting long-term fuel demand and optimizing the gas station network strategy. The approach combined macroeconomic modeling with statistical analysis to navigate the energy transition era.

1. Mid-to-Long Term Demand Forecasting

We developed forecasting models for various fuel types spanning a 30-year horizon.

  • Macro-Integration: Incorporated crude oil prices, vehicle ownership trends, and policy variables.
  • Structural Analysis: Identified statistical change points reflecting shifts in consumption patterns.

2. Fuel Mix & Structural Change Analysis

To understand shifts in the energy market share (Fuel Mix), we applied Compositional Data Analysis (CoDA) techniques.

Methodology:
  • Applied CLR (Centered Log-Ratio) transformation to handle closed-simplex share data.
  • Conducted PCA to interpret inter-fuel relationships and substitution effects.

This allowed us to link structural market shifts to external drivers such as EV adoption and environmental regulations.

3. Gas Station Network Optimization

Based on the demand forecasts, we estimated the optimal spatial distribution of gas stations to maximize network efficiency.

  • Supply-Demand Balance: Estimated regional gaps between fuel demand and station supply.
  • Cannibalization Analysis: Quantified negative impacts between adjacent stations within the network.
  • Decision Support: Established data-driven criteria for new station openings and strategic closures.

Conclusion & Impact

  • Provided actionable insights for long-term retail strategy under declining demand scenarios.
  • Enabled proactive responses to energy transition trends.
  • Optimized asset allocation by identifying low-efficiency sites and high-potential opening spots.
