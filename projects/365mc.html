Project Overview

This project was conducted in collaboration with 365mc, a leading Korean medical institution specializing in liposuction and obesity treatment. The objective was to identify key factors driving consultation-to-visit conversion and to design data-driven consultation guidelines.

1. Problem Definition

Despite high demand, significant performance variations existed across the consultation team.

  • Performance Gaps: Large disparity in conversion rates between top and bottom consultants.
  • Dependency on Experience: Quality relied heavily on individual know-how rather than standardized protocols.
  • Data Quality Issues: Existing STT data suffered from speaker-mixing and domain-specific transcription errors.

2. Analytical Approach & Pipeline

2-1. Data Pipeline Engineering
  • Built a Speaker Diarization + STT pipeline optimized for Korean medical consultations.
  • Applied LLM-based post-processing to correct medical terminology errors.
2-2. Multi-Perspective Analysis

Consultation data was analyzed across three distinct dimensions to isolate success factors:

  • Customer Metadata: Demographics and history.
  • Voice Features: Tone, speed, and silence intervals.
  • Text Analysis (STT): Keyword usage and conversation structure.

3. Key Insights

3-1. Conversion Drivers

Explicit appointment proposals increased conversion rates by over 18 percentage points. Strategies such as "schedule first, change later" significantly lowered customer resistance.

3-2. Negative Signals
  • Excessive interruptions by the consultant.
  • Overly long consultation durations without clear closing.

Conclusion & Outcome

  • Delivered actionable, data-driven consultation strategies.
  • Demonstrated that structured communication guidelines substantially improve reservation and visit rates.
  • Reduced dependency on implicit "gut feeling" by quantifying successful conversation patterns.
